Dr. Vishwanath, IAS appointed Secretary, MSME & Textiles, with additional charge as Managing Director, WBSIDCL

16Jun 2026

The Department of Personnel and Administrative Reforms, Government of West Bengal, has issued Notification No. 1135-PAR (IAS)/7P-215/11 appointing Dr. Vishwanath, IAS (WB:2010), Project Director, KEIIP in the rank of Secretary to the Government of West Bengal, as Secretary in the Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises and Textiles Department. The notification also assigns him additional charge as Managing Director, WBSIDCL, until further orders.

Budget Publication No. 4: Receipts under Consolidated Fund for 2026-2027

Jun2026

The Government of West Bengal's Budget Publication No. 4 presents receipts under the Consolidated Fund for the year 2026-2027. It includes actuals for 2024-2025, budget estimates for 2025-2026, revised estimates for 2025-2026, and budget estimates for 2026-2027 across revenue receipts, public debt, loans, and advances.

DocumentBudget Publication No. 4
CoverageReceipts under Consolidated Fund, 2026-2027
Relevant heads0851, 0852, and 6851
Read MSME-relevant highlights

Budget Publication No. 4
Receipts under Consolidated Fund for the year 2026-2027

The publication lists major heads including revenue receipts, grants-in-aid, public debt, loans, and advances. For CUTA members, the MSME-adjacent sections include 0851 - Village and Small Industries, 0852 - Industries, and 6851 - Loans for Village and Small Industries.

MSME-related references

Under 0851 - Village and Small Industries, the publication records receipts connected with industrial estates, small scale industries, handloom industries, handicrafts industries, khadi and village industries, sericulture industries, and other receipts.

Under 0852 - Industries, the publication includes entries for engineering industries, consumer industries, other industrial receipts, and related service or sale proceeds.

Under 6851 - Loans for Village and Small Industries, the publication includes loan receipts connected with small scale industries, handicrafts industries, public sector and other undertakings, and co-operatives.

Membership at CUTA

Be informed, connected, and represented.

Membership is open to eligible business owners, MSME units, entrepreneurs, manufacturers, traders, service providers, institutions, and professionals who share the Council's commitment to constructive enterprise development.

As a member, you become part of a serious business platform focused on practical awareness, networking, representation, and access to relevant information. Active participation helps the Council understand industry concerns and take them forward in a structured manner.

Member benefits

  • MSME-focused seminars and awareness programmes
  • Relevant notices, schemes, meetings, and updates
  • Connections with experienced enterprise leaders
  • A collective forum for practical business concerns
  • Visibility through approved member listings and activities
  • A platform that values responsible business conduct
Membership categories

A place for every enterprise contributor.

Final category terms, fees, and approval conditions are governed by the Council's official rules.

General Member

For MSME owners, proprietors, partners, directors, and entrepreneurs who wish to participate in Council activities.

Associate Member

For professionals, consultants, advisors, and service providers connected with MSME development.

Corporate / Institutional

For companies, trade bodies, associations, and institutions engaging with the Council's MSME network.

Honorary Member

For eminent persons and industry contributors invited by the Council in recognition of their support or guidance.

Eligibility

Before you apply.

  • Be connected with business, manufacturing, trade, services, entrepreneurship, professional practice, or MSME development.
  • Agree to follow the Council's rules, code of conduct, and membership conditions.
  • Provide accurate details, documents, and declarations where requested.
  • Understand that approval is subject to review by the Council's authorised representatives.
Code of conduct

Responsible participation.

  • Maintain respectful conduct in Council meetings, events, and communications.
  • Do not misuse the Council's name, logo, letterhead, or communication channels.
  • Do not represent the Council externally without formal authorisation.
  • Follow applicable laws, ethical commercial practices, and membership requirements.
